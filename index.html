The mesosphere is the third layer of Earth's
atmosphere, and it is the coldest layer, with
temperatures that can reach as low as -90 degrees Celsius
(-130 degrees Fahrenheit). The thermosphere is the hottest layer of Earth's atmosphere,
with temperatures that can reach up to 2,000 degrees Celsius
(3,632 degrees Fahrenheit). However, because the air in the
thermosphere is so thin, it would feel very cold to a human being. The Stratosphere holds the ozone layer:

Ozone is the layer of three oxygen atoms that shields
the earth's surface from the sun's harsh rays.
The stratosphere also contains very dry air that
is roughly 1000 times thinner than the air at sea level.

Crust The Earth's crust is the thinnest layer of the Earth,
but it is also the most diverse.
It is made up of many different types of rocks,
including igneous, metamorphic, and sedimentary rocks.
Mantle The mantle is the hottest layer of the Earth's solid interior,
reaching temperatures of up to 4,000 degrees Fahrenheit (2,200 degrees Celsius).
However, it is also the thickest layer, at about 2,900 kilometers (1,800 miles) thick.
Core
(inner and outer core) The inner core of the Earth is so hot that it could melt iron,
but it is solid because of the immense pressure at that depth.
The pressure is so great that it is equivalent to about 3.6 million
atmospheres, which is about 360,000 times the pressure at sea level.
The outer core of the Earth is liquid because the temperature is so high,
about 5,400 degrees Fahrenheit (2,900 degrees Celsius).
The liquid outer core is what creates the Earth's magnetic field.
The movement of the liquid metal in the outer core creates electric
currents, which in turn create the magnetic field.

That's more than all the known species of land animals combined! Scientists believe
that there are many more species of ocean animals that we haven't even discovered yet.
